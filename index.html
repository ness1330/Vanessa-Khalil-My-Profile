    Project Idea

    Overview

    A website or App which I would call “Buddy.” A website/app that is meant to assist people from suffering mental illness or for people that just need someone to talk to or feel that they need a friend.

    Motivation

    In 2017 the suicide rate in Australia was 12.7% per 100,000 deaths. With debt levels rising across Australia mental health is predicted to decline and suicide rates to rise by up to 40% by 2030. After recently losing a very close friend and an Uncle to suicide this holds close to my heart.

    Since technology is advancing rapidly, more and more people are doing things online, we are becoming more and more absent from real life human interaction. As time moves on, the less we will be physically engaging with people, meeting new people and having conversations with strangers will eventually significantly reduce further, its inevitable. While it is not necessarily a bad thing, the strength of our mental health relies on human to human interaction, we are social beings and strive on communication with each other.

    Description

  • It is free to join and offers free services of counselling and friendship.
  • The services will be provided by volunteer counsellors.
  • The idea is for people suffering a mental illness can jump on and have access to someone to talk to or engage in activities that can make them feel better.
  • My aim would be to encourage the people in the community also to join and befriend people. The people who volunteer their time can be an ear to listen and shoulder to cry on.
  • Similar concept to the Big Brother idea where a “Big Brother” is loaned to a child who needs help, Buddy will provide adults and teenagers a “Safe Zone”
  • Ideally the communication will be online, however, it can be up to the discretion of each party should they feel they would like to meet up at a safe location.
  • I propose that there will be different sections available as resources for engagement such as: A private section - (for people and counsellors only), this section will be strictly confidential and ideally between the counsellor and the user only.
  • A book club section – Where parties can group together, choose a book and then discuss the book. Just like a real-life book club. Reading is great for people for building imaginations and for stimulating the brain. Discussing the book within a group environment can help people engage and encourage conversation that is not just about a person that is suffering.
  • Games section – Simple one on one games such as online chess or cards, checkers etc. Like the book club section, encourages engagement and stimulates the brain. There can also be a solo games section for the users that just need to play by themselves.
  • Group Chat Section – Similar to a forum but groups can be created in this section, where certain topics can be discussed.
  • Fitness section – Fitness and exercise is great for releasing endorphins into the brain which can make you feel happier. Exercise helps with memory and keeping the brain healthy as well as helps maintain a healthy weight and lifestyle. There will be activities and goals in this section to encourage fitness. Users can track their exercise and activities in a diary.
  • Cooking Section – like exercise cooking is great for releasing endorphins, particularly when a ‘Master Chef’ dish is created. Users can share recipes ideas, share photos of their achievements.
  • y idea of this app/website is to help people suffering a mental illness or provide a way for people who want to provide help but don’t have the means to, to people who need the help.
  • The app/webpage will be heavily watched to ensure that there are not people on there bullying or generally causing issues. Buddy needs to be a safe zone and this sort of behaviour will not be tolerated.
